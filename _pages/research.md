# Publications

<div class="project-card">

  <div class="project-card__title">
    <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11318902" target="_blank">
      Impact of Environmental Factors on LoRa 2.4 GHz Time-of-Flight Ranging Outdoors
    </a>
  </div>

  
 <div class="project-card__meta">
    25-28 Nov 2025 <br>
    Published in:
    <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/xpl/conhome/11318850/proceeding" target="_blank">
      2025 Thirteenth International Symposium on Computing and Networking Workshops (CANDARW)
    </a><br>
    Conference Location: Yamagata, Japan <br>
    Authors: Y. Zhou, X. Zhou, Z. Cheng, C. Lu, <strong>J. Chen</strong>, J. Pan, Y. Liu, S. Li, and K. S. Kim
  </div>

  <div class="project-card__body">
    Research output from the SURF project on GPS-free geolocation. This work investigates the impact of environmental factors on outdoor LoRa 2.4 GHz RF Time-of-Flight ranging for high-precision localization in WSN/IoT applications. Experimental data was collected across a 400 m<sup>2</sup> outdoor grid, integrating temperature and humidity measurements with ranging records. Preliminary results using a deep neural network (DNN) demonstrate that environmental conditions significantly influence ranging accuracy, highlighting the need for compensation algorithms in GPS-free localization systems.
  </div>
